Introduction

Hello there! Throughout this page we are going to explore the city of Copenhagen and it's residents based on their age, city district, income, sex, etc.

The data used in this webpage is retrieved from opendata.dk. You can head there and find even more data about Copenhagen and Denmark. We're going to learn about the differences that are within the city of Copenhagen through exploratory analysis and of course a lot of cool graphs to make it easy to swallow.

The main dataset we're using consists of 4 features with categorical values. You can check out the different values in the following table.

That's a mouthful. But don't fret here is a visualization to ease it up a bit. It is a plot of the different districts, with the colors mapping to the number of people living in a district, and if you hover over the visualization you'll see the number of people in the district.

That is nice to play around with but it doesn't convey information as precisely as a column- or bar chart can and for that reason: Here is a column chart of the same data.
It is clear to spot in which district there lives the fewest people. With the combined knowledge from the map which shows the relative area between the districts and the column chart we can learn that even though Nørrebro is the smallest district it has the highest person count.

Differences in the city's districts

This is an interactive column chart combined with a map. You can click the various categories and hover over the districts to see the percentage of people represented in each category.

Use it to explore Copenhagen for yourself. There are many interesting tendencies to notice such as the many large residences in the center of Copenhagen ("Indre by") which is known it's high price per square meter. Also the many private housing cooperatives on Vesterbro and the few in Brønshøj-Husum
